# Manumation Vocabulary

A shared vocabulary keeps the team from building twelve versions of the same idea while pretending this is innovation. Use these terms consistently.

## Manumation

The discipline of designing human-AI systems that improve judgment, memory, decision quality, and execution without removing human responsibility.

## The Intelligence Layer

The product layer that sits between business tools, humans, AI models, agents, memory, and workflows.

It governs how information becomes better decisions.

## Donna

The primary interface and governance operator for the Intelligence Layer.

Donna coordinates agents, retrieves memory, applies the Manumation Evolution Loop, evaluates confidence, manages approvals, and helps humans make better decisions.

Donna is not the whole product. Donna is the face and control layer.

## Decision Object

The atomic unit of the Intelligence Layer.

A structured record of how a meaningful decision was made, what evidence supported it, what risks existed, who approved it, what happened afterward, and what should be learned.

## Manumation Evolution Loop

The quality-control loop used before meaningful answers, recommendations, actions, or executions.

The loop moves through intent, hidden goal, memory, evidence, assumptions, options, critique, confidence, approval, execution, and learning.

## Cognitive Quality Control

The practice of improving the quality of AI-assisted thinking before generating outputs or taking action.

This is the difference between producing an answer and producing a trustworthy recommendation.

## AI Governance

Rules, thresholds, permissions, approvals, audit history, and tenant-specific policies that determine what AI may do, suggest, or execute.

## Agent Mob

The specialist agent team Donna can route work to.

Examples: Research Agent, Builder Agent, Critic Agent, Teacher Agent, Sales Agent, Marketing Agent, Verifier Agent, Memory Agent.

## Memory Engine

The system responsible for retrieving relevant past decisions, client facts, project history, documents, notes, and relationship context.

Memory is scoped by tenant, project, department, user permission, and data classification.

## Governance Engine

The system responsible for deciding whether an action is safe to recommend, auto-execute, block, or send for human approval.

## Confidence Score

A 0-100 estimate of how trustworthy a recommendation is, based on context quality, evidence quality, contradiction checks, risk level, and missing information.

## Approval Gate

A required human checkpoint before the system executes an action or finalizes a recommendation.

Approval gates are mandatory for high-risk work, low-confidence work, external communication, sensitive data, payments, deletions, legal decisions, medical advice, and tenant configuration changes.

## Outcome Learning

The process of updating the Decision Object after execution so the system learns what happened and improves future recommendations.

## Release Candidate

A draft, answer, feature, or decision that has passed the Manumation Evolution Loop and is ready for human review or execution.
